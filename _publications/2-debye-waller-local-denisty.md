**Abstract.** This molecular dynamics simulation study of a glass-forming binary mixture and a FENE polymer sheds light on the structural origin of slow relaxation in these two glass formers. We show that the growth of the highest peak of the radial distribution function g(r) and the variation in density relaxation are correlated — related to the free volume available for density relaxation in free-volume theory. We also compare the growth of the surface density at the first peak to the Debye–Waller factor, another representation of free volume, which is connected to the local density by a power law in the moderately supercooled regime in these two different systems.

**Keywords:** free-volume theory, glass transitions, computer simulation, classical statistical mechanics.
